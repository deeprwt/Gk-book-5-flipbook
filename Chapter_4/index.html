4. Gifts of nature

15

Tardigrades, also known as water bears, are microscopic animals. They can survive extreme temperatures and can remain without water for up to 10 years. They can even survive in outer space!

Known as the 'golden spice of India', turmeric is often used as a medicine to treat wounds and infections because of its powerful anti-inflammatory and pain-reducing characteristics.

Rafflesia arnoldii, popularly known as the corpse flower, is the world's largest flower. It smells of rotting flesh and can weigh up to 11kg. It has no leaves, no roots, and no stem. It is found in the Southeast Asian islands of Sumatra and Borneo.

The Jakobshavn Glacier in Greenland has set a record by travelling 150 feet per day. It is the fastest moving glacier in the world.

The bark of the willow tree has a chemical called salicin which is used to make aspirin. Ancient Egyptians used the bark of the tree to reduce pain and aches.
