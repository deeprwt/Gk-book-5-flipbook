11. Indiaꞌs pride

There are three divisions of the Indian Defence forces—Army, Navy, and Air Force. The head of all the combined Defence forces is the President of India.

The Indian army is the land-based branch of our defence services.

The Indian Navy is the water-based branch of our defence services.

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air-based branch of our defence services.

Did you know

'Operation Rahat' was one of the biggest civilian rescue operations ever carried out in the world. It was carried out by the Indian Air Force when they rescued civilians from the Uttarakhand floods in 2013.

The Republic Day parade is held every year on 26th January at Rajpath, New Delhi. It is a three-day celebration where the Indian defence capabilities are presented. A foreign dignitary, who is generally a head of a country, is invited as the chief guest. The celebrations end with the 'Beating Retreat' ceremony held at Vijay Chowk, New Delhi on 29th January, where the bands of the defence forces play together. It is a sight worth seeing!

There are several divisions of the Central Armed Police Force (CAPF) who deal with internal threats. These are:

Assam Rifles (AR)

Border Security Force (BSF)

Central Industrial Security Force (CISF)

Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF)

Indo Tibetan Border Police (ITBP)

National Security Guard (NSG)

Sashastra Seema Bal (SSB)
