There is a special Olympics for winter sports held once every four years. Let's read and find out more about them!

Biathlon is a sport where competitors have to cover a cross-country course, carrying a specially designed rifle. The skill of participants is tested in two events: cross-country skiing and rifle shooting.

Curling is a unique game played on ice, where participants have to guide a slab of stone to a target area. It is also known as 'chess on ice.'

Snowboarding evolved from skateboarding and surfing. Participants go down snow-covered slopes on a board which is attached to their feet.

Ice hockey is a fast-paced game and is a variation of field hockey. It is played on ice using a 'puck' which is the equivalent of a hockey ball.

Luge is a dangerous sport where participants lie on a tiny sled with their legs outstretched. They race down icy tracks at very high speeds, manoeuvring with their body

In ski jumping, participants ski down a slope and jump off the edge to land. The skiers are judged on the jump made, the distance covered, and their form in mid-air.
